Need Help?

The point of PROVisualizer is to give first time researchers a high-level view of the archives.

Click on the dropdown menu under Function (activity of government) to browse the areas of government we hold records about or try a keyword search. You can filter any results by entering a year.

A blue circle represents an activity of government (Victorian Function) we hold records about. If it helps, you can think of functions as Topics.
A yellow circle represents a government agency or body (Victorian Agency) responsible for administering an activity (function) of government. Clicking on an agency label will take you to a list of records created by the agency that we preserve. There are many agencies connected to the same government function because responsibility for it has changed over time. Since 1836 different agencies have been responsible for different activities of government, and transfer the records they create to us for long-term preservation.

The darker and bigger a circle, the more connections it has to other functions and or agencies and might be a good place to start your research journey.

When you click on an agency label you will see a list of records (Victorian Series) created by that agency.

When you roll your cursor over a circle, a label will appear identifying that function or agency. Click on the label to learn more about it in our online catalogue on a separate page.

If you are looking at a highly connected function or agency you might need to move things around to get a better view. Hereâ€™s the best way to do this:

  1. Roll your mouse over a function or agency. When you see the cursor turn into cross arrows click and drag the function or agency circle to another part of the screen. Anything connected to it will come along for the ride!
  2. Click on any empty space on the screen then drag to move the entire visualisation.
  3. Click on any empty space on the screen then double click to zoom in You can zoom in and out by also rolling your mouse wheel.

Just click on the sharing icon in the lower left of the visualisation. You can even embed the whole visualisation along with all its functionality into your own web page with a simple copy and paste of the embed code we provide!
